Artistic Skills & Mediums

"I dream my painting and then I paint my dream" - Vincent van Gogh.

I've had the opportunity to work with a variety of traditional art mediums over the years, including colored pencils, watercolor, acrylics, and oils. While I currently focus on watercolor and acrylic pieces — with the occasional colored pencil drawing — my experience with each medium has shaped my technique and given me a strong foundation in portrait work.

Another key skill I bring to my art is working closely with people. I love listening to a client's vision and helping bring it to life. Every piece begins with a conversation — I ask for a few reference photos and gather input on posing, personal touches, and preferences. From there, I create a digital sketch and share it for feedback. After any revisions, I send a final draft for approval before starting the painting process.

For me, the skill isn't just in the brushwork — it's in understanding and capturing a story. My goal is always to create artwork that's both personal and meaningful, and to make sure each client feels seen in the final piece.
